My educational philosophy is deeply rooted in my own cross-cultural journey. Growing up in international school environments (GSIS, Tanglin, Taipei British School) gave me a firsthand appreciation for diverse perspectives.

This experience fostered a deep cultural humility—a core counseling trait. It informs my work with minority families and my approach to inclusive education, ensuring that every learner feels seen, valued, and understood regardless of their background.

The route reads like a passport: stamped in Taipei, renewed in Singapore, settled in Hong Kong — first as a translator between languages at CUHK SCE, then between people: a psychologist at HKBU, and finally a guidance counsellor at CUHK.

▪CH. 01 § THE LEDGER

Teaching Impact & Stories

Psychology & Sociology

Maria College

Mentoring with Empathy: Beyond delivering lectures, I mentored students in the "Student Relations" program. I used my own counseling experience to show them how to handle real-world cases, turning abstract theories into practical empathy.

Vocational Education

Institute of Vocational Education (IVE)

Holistic Career Development: Focused on student development and soft skills. I designed interactive workshops that addressed student anxieties about the future, bridging the gap between academic study and workplace reality with reassurance and clarity.

Project Management & Operations

Workplace Training

Empowering Teams: As a Project Officer, I trained teams on workplace software. My goal was to reduce workplace stress by making efficiency accessible, understanding group dynamics to turn "boring" admin tasks into streamlined workflows.

Autism Child Foundations

SEN Teaching

Individualized Care: Provided specialised support for children with autism. This role taught me unconditional positive regard and the importance of observing non-verbal cues to tailor learning strategies.
