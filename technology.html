▪CH. 04 § Origin — The Line of Descent

Narratives & Journey

1

▪2015 • Origin Story

The Reality Check

"I thought I was tech-savvy because I could design slides. Then I failed an interview because I couldn't do a VLOOKUP. I was humbled. That failure sparked a vow: never be incompetent again. I didn't just learn Excel; I mastered its very DNA."

Humbled
It wasn't about "playing games" vs "work." It was realizing I only knew the surface. That shame drove me to understand the mechanisms behind the tools, laying the groundwork for every system I've built since.
2

▪2017 • First Build

The First System

"200 active cases. No AI. No shortcuts. Just me, Stack Overflow, and 6 months of brute-force learning."

Grit
I built a VBA-powered "One-Stop Portal" from scratch at ISSHK. It required learning SQL queries and complex macros on the fly. It was the most painful learning curve of my life, but it transformed a chaotic workflow into a precision engine for my team.
3

▪2018–2020 • Levelling Up

Scaling Complexity

"That first victory proved I could build anything if I dug deep enough. The tools just got better."

Evolution
At EdUHK's SEC Department, I built the SEC Departmental OSP — a full workflow automation suite for teacher training programmes. Same "mechanism-first" mindset, but now with Python, PowerQuery, and proper database design.
4

▪2023–2025 • Enterprise Architecture

The 4Set Evolution

A 4-generation journey from a fragile Excel monolith to a distributed hybrid-cloud infrastructure. Born from the "Data Starvation" of Gen 0, refined by the "Sneakernet" PWA of Gen 1, saved by the "Ghost Monitor" of Gen 2, and perfected by the async power of Gen 3.

Gen 0: Excel Gen 1: PWA Gen 2: Python Gen 3: Server

The Catalyst

"The Qualtrics API was horrible. 1-second polling per record. It locked the UI and made mass uploads impossible."

Gen 2: Synchronous Polling (Fragile)
Gen 3: Async Background Daemon (Robust)
5

▪2025 • Social Innovation

Beyond the Workplace

For years, my systems focused on making teams faster, calmer, and more reliable. In 2025, I started pushing that same mindset outward — building for the community, not just the organisation.

Equal Play • Awardee BESGO FoundRise Assistive Tech AI + Games

Equal Play (2025)

A musical/rehab gadget: a glove with gentle air-bag guidance for hand posture, paired with an AI-powered piano score writer. The goal is to help children build piano habits through fun games, guided practice, and AI-generated learning content.

Supported through the CUHK Business School BESGO FoundRise (GBA Youth Entrepreneurship Support Scheme).
